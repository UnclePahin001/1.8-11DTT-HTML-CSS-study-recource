Mathmatics | 1.12 Chance & Data

NCEA Level One Mathmatics

The Chance and Data strand focuses on chance events and data-handling processes. In Chance, the focus is on developing your ability to make predictions about how likely an event is in situations where there is uncertainty. In Data, the focus is on collecting, organizing, analyzing, and presenting the information. As a result of your learning, you will be able to use your understanding of likelihood to compare and order everyday chance events and be able to answer questions using data collection and/or interpretation of real-life contexts.

Achievement

Demonstrate an understanding of chance and data

Make sure you:
- demonstrate an understanding of chance and data
- use a range of appropriate concepts and terms to demonstrate an understanding of statistical literacy and probability
- you should be familiar with: the statistical enquiry cycle; reading and interpreting statistical tables, graphs and associated text; analysing statistical investigations; multivariate, bivariate and time series data; probability concepts
- evaluate statistical investigations or probability activities undertaken by others, including data collection methods, choice of measures, and validity of findings
- calculate probabilities, using fractions, percentages, and ratios
- evaluate statistical reports in the media by relating the displays, statistics, processes, and probabilities used to the claims made
- investigate situations that involve elements of chance: calculating probabilities in discrete situations

Achievement with Merit

Demonstrate an understanding of chance and data, justifying statements and findings

Make sure you:
- make sure that you can meet the criteria for achievement
- you need to demonstrate understanding of chance and data, justifying statements and findings
- provide supporting evidence such as summary statistics, probabilities, data values, trends or features of visual displays, and could involve reference to the context and the population

Achievement with Excellence

Demonstrate understanding of chance and data, showing statistical insight

Make sure you:
- make sure that you can meet the criteria for achievement and merit
- demonstrate understanding of chance and data, showing statistical insight
- integrate the statistical and contextual information and knowledge to show a deeper understanding
- provide critical reflection on the validity of the processes and conclusions given in contexts involving probability or statistics

Advice and tips

To make the absolute most of your exam and excel yourself,

Make sure you:
- you may be expected to complete probability trees and use these to find probabilities for any level of achievement
- multiply probabilities along the branches of tree diagrams if you are asked for the probability that an event AND another event occur
- add probabilities at the end of the branches if you are asked for the probability that an event OR another event occurs
- work in a logical order so you donâ€™t leave gaps
- attempt to answer every question, even if you are struggling to do so
- probabilities are best written as fractions but may be written as a decimal or percentage
- if written as a percentage, you must include the percentage sign
- probability must not be written as a ratio
- proofread for missed questions and confusing answers
- be familiar with outcomes based on using dice, coins, and playing cards
- read questions carefully and check that you have answered what is asked
- if you are asked to justify your reasons, a good way to answer clearly is to clearly bullet point your statements
- show all working as credit may be given for a minor slip, if the assessor can see what you have done
